Arithmetic Progression - An Introduction

Arithmetic progression is a sequence of numbers where each term is obtained by adding a fixed number to the previous term. This fixed number is called the common difference. For example, the sequence 2, 5, 8, 11, 14 is an arithmetic progression with a common difference of 3.

To find the nth term of an arithmetic progression, we use the formula:

an = a1 + (n-1)d

where:

  • an is the nth term
  • a1 is the first term
  • d is the common difference
  • n is the number of terms

Let's use this formula to find the 10th term of the sequence 3, 7, 11, 15, ...

a10 = 3 + (10-1)4

a10 = 39

So, the 10th term of the sequence is 39.

Arithmetic progression is a fundamental concept in mathematics and is used in various fields such as physics, engineering, and finance. By understanding the basics of arithmetic progression and the formula to find the nth term, you can solve various problems in these fields.
